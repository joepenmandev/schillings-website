# Ethics Framework — Schillings Intelligence

*Working draft. v0.1. Intended for public publication once partner-approved.*

---

## Why this exists

Schillings has spent forty years working at the intersection of reputation, privacy, and risk. The Schillings Intelligence platform is an extension of that practice into a new operating environment for executives, principals, and the institutions that surround them.

Premium technology in this space cannot be neutral. It must take a position on what it will and will not do — and that position must be visible.

This document is that position.

---

## Who we serve

We serve the people we have always served: chairs, founders, principals, families, and the senior advisors and institutions that act on their behalf. We extend our practice to those people through the platform, but we do not change who we serve.

The platform is not a mass-market product. We do not optimise for scale. We optimise for the quality of judgement we make available to the small number of people whose situations make our work necessary.

---

## Our principles

### 1. The principal is the only legitimate subject

The platform produces personalised assessments only for the principal who initiated the request, or for a third party whose authorised representative has attested to consent.

We do not allow anyone to score, profile, or assess a third party at scale. We refuse, by design, requests that attempt to do so.

### 2. Judgement over automation

Every Schillings Intelligence assessment is supported by signal-based analysis, but the final voice of the platform is a Schillings voice. The most premium feature is a human being. We do not let the technology run ahead of the people responsible for what it produces.

### 3. Confidence over certainty

The platform never claims more certainty than the evidence supports. Every Index carries a confidence band, named assumptions, and explicit limits. We treat uncertainty as a feature of intelligence work, not a vulnerability of a product.

### 4. Privacy by default

By default, the platform does not retain user-provided content beyond the duration of an assessment. Storage is opt-in and tied to specific functions (analyst review, monitoring, founding-client cohort calibration). Sensitive personal data is never used unless explicitly volunteered and consented to.

### 5. Non-advice in plain sight

The platform does not provide legal advice or regulatory advice. It produces intelligence assessments. Where a principal needs legal advice, the platform routes them — clearly and quickly — into Schillings's legal practice.

### 6. Refusal as a feature

We will refuse requests that fall outside the platform's principled boundaries. Refusals are not failures of the product; they are evidence of the product's seriousness. Each refusal explains the principle behind it and offers a constructive alternative path.

### 7. Independence of methodology

Methodologies are authored by Schillings, peer-reviewed by named external authorities, and versioned publicly. The methodologies are not influenced by the commercial preferences of any single client.

### 8. Transparency of provenance

Every output discloses, at the level of detail appropriate to the audience, which signals are deterministic, which are model-inferred, and which are analyst-reviewed. The provenance trail of a report — who and what shaped it — is part of the report.

### 9. No synthetic personas of real people

The platform does not generate synthetic media of real individuals. It does not produce voice, image, or text content that purports to be from a person who did not author it. This is a hard limit.

### 10. Accountability

The platform's editorial, methodological, and operational decisions are owned by named individuals at Schillings. The platform is not anonymous. Reports can be attributed to the partners who reviewed them.

---

## What the platform will not do

- It will not produce legal advice through an interface.
- It will not name third parties as adversaries.
- It will not assess minors.
- It will not generate synthetic media of real individuals.
- It will not retaliate, or recommend retaliation, against any individual or institution.
- It will not interfere, or recommend interference, with regulatory or judicial processes.
- It will not aggregate user data to derive profiles of non-consenting third parties.

---

## How we will be held accountable

- The Schillings Intelligence advisory board includes external authorities in privacy law, AI safety, behavioural science, OSINT, and security. The board reviews methodologies and ethics policy at least annually.
- A red-team programme tests the platform's outputs continuously for legal, reputational, and ethical failures.
- Methodology change logs are published. The history of how each Index has evolved is public.
- A clearly named editorial owner maintains the Voice and Tone Bible and the Output Safety Taxonomy.
- Users can request human review of any platform output, free of charge.
