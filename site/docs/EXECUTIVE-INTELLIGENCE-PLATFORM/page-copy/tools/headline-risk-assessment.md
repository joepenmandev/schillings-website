---

## What it answers

- How will a journalist, regulator, employee, investor, or activist read this?
- What is the worst plausible headline this could produce?
- How likely is this artifact to leave its intended boundary?
- Where does this touch legally sensitive ground (without crossing into legal advice)?

---

## What it produces

A **Public Interpretation Analysis** containing:

- Worst plausible headline.
- Screenshot virality risk.
- Internal leak severity.
- Legal sensitivity flags (directional, not advice).
- Media amplification probability.
- Stakeholder fallout risk.

Plus interpretive paragraphs through default audience lenses:

- Investigative journalist
- Senior employee
- Major investor or board member
- Activist coalition
- Sector regulator
- Adjacent peer or rival

Conditional lenses (gated): family-office staff, plaintiffs' counsel, hostile state-aligned media operator.

---

## Inputs

- Pasted text content (the artifact under assessment).
- Optional: intended audience, channel, deadline.
- Optional: principal identity to anchor exposure context.

Inputs are not retained beyond the session by default.

---

## What you receive

A unified Schillings Intelligence Report containing:

- Top-line verdict.
- Dimension panel.
- Audience lens panel.
- Confidence panel.
- AI provenance disclosure.
- Recommended priorities (rephrase, hold, escalate, route to advisor).
- Linked insights — particularly to the Executive Exposure Audit and the Narrative Escalation Map.
- Analyst review CTA.

---

## How it connects

- → Run the **Narrative Warfare Analyzer** to see how the framing could evolve over 7-30 days.
- → Run the **Executive Exposure Audit** to anchor the principal's broader posture.
- → Run the **Crisis Escalation Forecast** if the artifact is communications inside an active or anticipated incident.

---

## Privacy and refusal

- Inputs are not retained beyond the session by default.
- The Assessment refuses to assess content that targets named third parties with pejorative framing.
- The Assessment refuses content involving minors.
- The Assessment refuses content that itself appears to be unlawful.
