---

## What it answers

- Which framings in this material are most likely to be exploited?
- Which vectors are most likely to amplify the narrative?
- What are the most plausible next headlines?
- Which intervention points can reduce escalation risk?

---

## What it produces

A **Narrative Escalation Map** containing:

- Adversarial framing analysis.
- Pressure-point ranking.
- Emotional trigger analysis.
- Escalation pathways for days 1-7, 7-14, and 14-30.
- Narrative vectors by actor type.
- Amplification likelihood range.
- Likely next headlines with confidence.

Optional adversary lens rendering is available through Threat Actor archetypes.

---

## Inputs

- Pasted source content (article, thread, transcript, statement).
- Optional: principal identity to anchor exposure context.
- Optional: selected adversary archetypes from the Threat Actor Library.

Inputs are not retained beyond the session by default.

---

## What you receive

A unified Schillings Intelligence Report containing:

- Top-line synthesis and dominant escalation pathway.
- Pressure-point panel.
- Vector panel.
- Headline preview panel.
- Adversary lens panel (when selected).
- Confidence panel.
- AI provenance disclosure.
- Recommended priorities.
- Linked insights to Exposure and Crisis assessments.
- Analyst review CTA.

---

## How it connects

- → Run the **Headline Risk Assessment** to evaluate interpretation risk in a draft communication.
- → Run the **Executive Exposure Audit** to anchor narrative risk in broader principal exposure.
- → Run the **Reputation Crisis Simulator** when escalation appears likely within operational timeframes.

---

## Privacy and refusal

- Inputs are not retained beyond the session by default.
- The Assessment refuses requests that target named individuals with pejorative framing.
- The Assessment refuses requests to generate hostile content.
- Threat actors are rendered as archetypes only.
