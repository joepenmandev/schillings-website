## Hero

**Eyebrow.** Pillar — Narrative Intelligence

**Headline.** How communications are interpreted, framed, and amplified.

**Sub-headline.** A piece of writing is not what it says — it is what each audience hears. Narratives are not what they are now — they are what they become. The platform makes both visible.

---

## What it answers

- How will a journalist, regulator, employee, investor, or activist read this?
- Where would coverage of this go in the next 7-30 days, if it landed today?
- Which framings are latent in this piece — and where would they be exploited downstream?
- What headline is most likely to follow?

## How interpretation is rendered

- **Default audience lenses.** Investigative journalist, senior employee, major investor, activist coalition, regulator, adjacent peer.
- **Conditional lenses.** Family-office staff, plaintiffs' counsel, hostile state-aligned media operator (gated to Verified Principal or analyst-review tiers).
- **Adversary lens.** Re-rendering through any Threat Actor archetype.

**CTA.** Read the Public Interpretation Analysis methodology (v1.0) · Read the Narrative Escalation Map methodology (v1.0)

## Recommended journey

1. **Run the Headline Risk Assessment** on the artifact in question.
2. **Open the Narrative Escalation Map** to see how the framing could evolve.
3. **Open the Adversary Lens** for one or more Threat Actor archetypes.
4. **Consider analyst review.** A named Schillings partner returns signed observations within SLA.

---

## Privacy and refusal

- Inputs are not retained beyond the session by default.
- The platform refuses to assess content that targets named third parties with pejorative framing.
- The platform refuses to *generate* hostile content under any circumstances.
