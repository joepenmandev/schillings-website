# Editorial entity strategy (Phase 3A)

**Classification:** REVIEW REQUIRED (editorial, brand, marketing)  
**Purpose:** Over time, **reinforce** expertise areas, author authority, and recurring strategic themes through editorial choices — **without** repetitive keyword behaviour or sounding optimised.

**Related:** [`SEO-EDITORIAL-AUTHORITY-STRATEGY.md`](SEO-EDITORIAL-AUTHORITY-STRATEGY.md) (category → hub support), [`SEO-ANCHOR-VOCABULARY.md`](SEO-ANCHOR-VOCABULARY.md) (language zones by family).

---

## Goal

Editorial should help search engines and serious readers associate Schillings with:

- **Deep competence** in reputation, privacy, intelligence, high-stakes disputes, strategic communications, regulatory pressure, and cross-border complexity.  
- **Named authors** as credible voices (not generic bylines).  
- **Recurring themes** that match firm strategy — not a grab-bag of trends.

---

## Prioritise (conceptual, not mechanical)

| Lever | Application |
|-------|-------------|
| **Conceptual reinforcement** | Return to core themes with new developments — depth, not repetition. |
| **Narrative consistency** | Institutional worldview stays coherent across pieces (calm, precise, proportionate). |
| **Recurring expertise framing** | Pieces implicitly “belong” near known practice territories without aping hub copy. |
| **Authoritative terminology** | Use precise legal and strategic language naturally — see vocabulary doc for family-appropriate zones. |
| **Trust accumulation** | Fewer, stronger pieces that accumulate citations and memory. |

---

## Avoid

- **Keyword-heavy** recurrence of commercial head terms in editorial intros and subheads.  
- **Template intros** that read like SEO (“In today’s fast-paced world…”).  
- **Same phrase** in every article for a hub (signals automation or optimisation).  
- **Flattening** distinct expertise areas into one generic “risk” mush.

---

## Authors and entities

- Prefer **attributed** analysis where possible; house byline has a defined role (see `news-house-author` / editorial governance).  
- Author pages and bios should support **trust** and entity recognition — not compete for broad commercial queries (Phase 2 ownership model).  
- External reinforcement (directories, interviews, citations) compounds entity strength — see authority strategy §3.

---

## Measurement (qualitative + GCS)

- Watch for **author** and **theme** query expansion that reflects reputation, not spam.  
- Watch for **topic ownership stability** — editorial supports hubs; see [`SEO-MEASUREMENT-FRAMEWORK.md`](SEO-MEASUREMENT-FRAMEWORK.md) (Phase 3A editorial metrics).
