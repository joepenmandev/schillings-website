**Purpose:** Shift from **architecture** (Phases 1–2) to **earned authority**: topical depth, trust, distinctiveness, and stable SERP roles — **without** turning Intelligence into an SEO blog or multiplying landing pages.

## 1. Intelligence as an authority engine

Intelligence is **not** a traffic blog. It is a **trust and authority layer** that:

- Supports **expertise** hubs with depth and credibility (informationally, not as disguised service copy).
- Earns **citations**, links, and source recognition over time.
- Ranks **informationally** for interpretive and news-adjacent queries where appropriate.
- Reinforces **entities** (firm, authors, themes) in a premium, restrained register.
- Builds **topical breadth** without flattening commercial intent onto articles.

**Hard rule:** No hard commercial funneling from editorial body copy. Discovery paths stay **natural** (navigation, contextual bridges already governed in Phase 2).

## A. Editorial role categories

| Category | Typical focus | Premium constraint |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Reactive analysis** | Breaking developments, fast clarity | Original framing — not wire rewrites |
| **Legal interpretation** | What changed, what it implies for clients | Expert perspective — not generic explainers |
| **Reputation risk analysis** | Narrative, stakeholders, record | Calm authority — not alarm copy |
| **Investigations / intelligence insight** | Method, limits, decision-useful insight | Source sophistication — no “spy thriller” tone |
| **Geopolitical commentary** | Cross-border exposure, sanctions, politics of risk | Measured — not hot takes as default |
| **Crisis response analysis** | Rhythm, discipline, trade-offs | Concise expert analysis — not playbook listicles |
| **Privacy / security developments** | Law, technology, human impact | Differentiated viewpoint — not news summaries only |
| **Digital threat analysis** | Platforms, coercion, misinformation, AI | Non-commodity — avoid “ultimate guide” packaging |

## C. Editorial standards (non-negotiable for Phase 3 quality)

**Require**

- Original framing and a clear **why this, why now, why us**.
- Expert perspective — attributable judgment, proportionate claims.
- Non-generic insights (if it could appear on any firm blog, reject or rewrite).
- Source sophistication: primary references, proportionate quoting, no astroturfing.
- Premium tone: calm, precise, confident, **restrained**.

**Avoid**

- SEO-blog formatting as the default shape (snackable headings that exist only for skimming).
- Generic explainers and textbook definitions without a Schillings angle.
- Listicle structures where a short essay would carry more authority.
- Obvious keyword targeting, repetitive head-term phrasing, “traffic first” intros.
- Inflated volume: shallow commentary published for cadence alone.

## 2. Original research and insight layer

Strongest long-term signals are **non-commodity** work. High-value **future** shapes (commission only when strategically justified):

- Annual or periodic **reputation risk** outlook (methodology transparent, tone sober).
- **Crisis** trend analysis (patterns, not anecdotes-as-data).
- **Online threat** pattern analysis (law + behaviour + platforms).
- **AI / disinformation** monitoring notes (evidence-led, bounded claims).
- **Cross-border disputes** trends (coordination, forums, narrative risk).
- **Regulatory pressure** trends (enforcement climate, not generic compliance tips).
- **Media risk** intelligence (how scrutiny behaves — not “PR tips”).
- **Digital identity** risk analysis (proportionate, privacy-respecting framing).

**Goal:** Become **reference-worthy** — citable, linkable, defensible — not merely “optimised.”

## 3. Entity expansion outside the site

Internal architecture is necessary but not sufficient. **Authority compounds faster off-site** when:

- Named authors accumulate **citations**, directory profiles, and recognised bios.
- The firm earns **high-trust mentions** (press, legal directories, podcasts, conferences).
- **Backlinks** reflect real relationships and expertise, not purchased placements.
- Knowledge graph and brand entities strengthen through **consistent naming** and recognised sources (not schema hacks — governance already restricts schema churn).

**Rule:** External work should point **back** to legitimate homes (firm, people, Intelligence) where it aids users — never to manufactured URL farms.

## 4. SERP role consolidation (target model)

Each family should **dominate a different SERP role**; overlap is managed, not ignored.

| Family | Target SERP role |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Expertise** | Commercial + transactional **authority** (retained counsel, practice discovery) |
| **Situations** | High-intent **contextual** discovery (scenario-shaped queries) |
| **Intelligence** | **Informational** authority + thought leadership |
| **People** | **Trust** validation + entity reinforcement (not broad commercial heads) |
| **Contact / offices** | **Conversion** + local / routing intent |
| **About** | **Brand** legitimacy and institutional narrative |

**Avoid:** Multiple types competing for the **same** role (e.g. articles or topic archives replacing hubs for commercial practice queries). See [`SEO-QUERY-OWNERSHIP-AUDIT.md`](SEO-QUERY-OWNERSHIP-AUDIT.md) for risk flags.

## 5. Query consolidation (active monitoring)

Use [`SEO-MEASUREMENT-FRAMEWORK.md`](SEO-MEASUREMENT-FRAMEWORK.md) and GSC.

**Good signals**

- Expertise hubs gain **broader query sets** without trashy tail.
- Articles **support** discovery and citations; hubs retain commercial ownership.
- Situations **assist** topical relevance without fragmenting head commercial intent.
- People support **trust** and branded / long-tail entity queries.

**Bad signals**

- Articles **outrank** commercial hubs for practice-class queries.
- Situations **fragment** commercial queries across many URLs.
- Profiles rank for **broad** non-branded practice keywords at scale.
- Topic archives behave as **pseudo-hubs** (commercial tone, CTA-shaped framing).

**Key success signal (behavioural + qualitative):** Users find **Intelligence** first for insight, then move toward **expertise** through normal navigation — not forced funnel copy.

## 6. Editorial sophistication (culture)

Intelligence should feel **more authoritative than optimised**.

**Prioritise:** Insight density, sophisticated framing, premium tone, concise expert analysis, differentiated viewpoints, calm authority.

**Deprioritise:** Template-driven SEO shapes, repetitive keyword phrasing, “ultimate guides,” traffic-blog cadence, shallow commentary.

---

## 7. Search Console and entity monitoring

Track (aligned with measurement framework):

- Query **ownership** stability vs intended primary URLs.
- Entity / query **expansion** for firm and named experts (branded and semi-branded).
- **Assisted** paths: informational queries landing on Intelligence with later engagement on expertise (analytics + qualitative).
- Author query **emergence** (people + author archives — trust, not commercial poaching).
- Expertise **topical breadth** (hub-level, not keyword stuffing).
- Informational → commercial **transitions** (segment carefully — avoid false precision).

---

## 8. Content gap strategy (when to commission)

Identify **missing authority territory**:

- Recurring queries with **no** clear on-site owner (see audit doc).
- Emerging legal / reputation / security themes with **thin** coverage.
- **Unowned** informational SERP categories where Schillings has a **defensible** point of view.

**Create content only when:**

- Strategically valuable and **authority-building**.
- **Non-commodity** (clear angle, expert voice).
- Aligned with **family intent** (editorial stays editorial).

**Do not:** SEO landing-page proliferation, keyword–city combinations, AI article scaling, content-velocity targets, FAQ inflation, topical-map spam, templates optimised for traffic first.

---

## Long-term target (search + brand)

Search engines and users should infer:

- Schillings is a **recognised authority** in its chosen topics.
- **Expertise** hubs define commercial territory.
- **Intelligence** demonstrates real expertise and judgment.
- **People** reinforce credibility.
- The **brand** owns sophisticated, high-trust themes.

**Without** the property visibly resembling a maximally engineered SEO site.
