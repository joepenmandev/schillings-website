# Content lifecycle governance (Phase 3A)

**Classification:** REVIEW REQUIRED (editorial, digital, partnership)  
**Purpose:** **When** Intelligence (and related) content should stay live, be refreshed, merged, retired, or stop receiving active internal reinforcement — so the archive stays **selective and credible**, not an ever-growing SEO footprint.

**Related:** [`SEO-EDITORIAL-QUALITY-STANDARD.md`](SEO-EDITORIAL-QUALITY-STANDARD.md), [`SEO-MEASUREMENT-FRAMEWORK.md`](SEO-MEASUREMENT-FRAMEWORK.md).

---

## Principle

**Not every article should live forever as an active authority surface.**

Stale, wrong, or thin pieces **dilute** perceived quality and can confuse ownership signals. Lifecycle decisions are **editorial and strategic**, not a technical purge for its own sake.

---

## States and actions

| State | When | Typical action |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **Evergreen** | Accurate, still cited, still reflects firm judgment | Keep; light factual checks on schedule. |
| **Update** | Still relevant but law, facts, or context moved | Revise body, date note if policy allows, preserve URL where possible. |
| **Consolidate** | Multiple pieces overlap one theme weakly | Merge into one authoritative piece; redirect or retire duplicates per tech/governance process. |
| **Retire** | Misleading, obsolete, or below current bar | Unpublish or noindex per firm policy; avoid orphan internal links (site hygiene). |
| **Stop active reinforcement** | Piece stays for record but should not be spotlighted | Remove from featured slots, reduce cross-linking from hubs if it was ever linked ad hoc; do not “promote” in navigation. |

---

## Triggers to review

- **Material legal or factual change** affecting the thesis.  
- **Quality bar drift** — piece no longer passes sophistication check.  
- **Cannibalisation** — several URLs compete for the same informational intent; consolidate.  
- **Brand risk** — tone or claims no longer acceptable.  
- **Performance reality** — zero engagement and no citation value over a long window *and* low quality (judgment call, not analytics alone).

---

## What not to do

- **Mass delete** for indexation games without editorial and compliance review.  
- **Automatic** retirement from dashboards only (traffic is a hint, not the decision).  
- **Redirect chains** or route changes without following protected systems (Appendix A, hreflang, canonical governance).

*Technical implementation of redirects/noindex is out of scope for this document — follow site governance when executing.*
