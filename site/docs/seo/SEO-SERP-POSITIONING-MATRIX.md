## 2. “Ideal SERP perception” narratives

### Strategic (chapter-like)

- **Perception:** “This is how Schillings structures serious problems — calm, integrated, premium.”  
- **Avoid:** Cheap superlatives; fake urgency on non-crisis pages.

### Editorial / Intelligence

- **Perception:** “This is how the firm *thinks* — elite, measured, legally literate.”  
- **Avoid:** CTA language in titles; commercial keywords in article titles without editorial approval.

### Profiles

- **Perception:** “Real people I could instruct — credible, specific, appropriately discreet.”  
- **Avoid:** Salesy bios; keyword lists in names/titles.

### Conversion

- **Perception:** “I can reach the firm safely and efficiently.”  
- **Avoid:** SEO paragraphs above the fold; widening layout “for SEO.”

### Legal / compliance

- **Perception:** “Legitimate, boring-in-a-good-way institutional pages.”  
- **Avoid:** Marketing fluff.

---

## 3. CTR tactics (governance-safe)

1. **Honest specificity** in meta (jurisdiction, practice, problem) beats generic polish.  
2. **Regional markers** where pages are truly regional (Contact, About, home).  
3. **Problem-first** leading clause for situation pages — test in small batches.  
4. **Do not** promise outcomes (“win your case”, “destroy the story”) in snippets.

---

## 4. Risk classes for SERP experiments

- **SAFE:** Meta-only tests on one URL; before/after GSC CTR 4-week windows.  
- **REVIEW REQUIRED:** Title pattern tests on a family template.  
- **HIGH RISK:** Homepage or Intelligence index rewrites (brand + editorial gates).  
- **CRITICAL:** Schema, hreflang, canonical — **out of bounds** here.
