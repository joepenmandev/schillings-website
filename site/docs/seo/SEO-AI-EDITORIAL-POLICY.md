# AI usage — editorial policy (Phase 3A)

**Classification:** REVIEW REQUIRED (editorial, legal/compliance as applicable, partnership)  
**Purpose:** Clear rules for **assistive** use of AI in editorial work — without outsourcing authority, judgment, or brand voice to generation systems.

**Related:** [`SEO-EDITORIAL-QUALITY-STANDARD.md`](SEO-EDITORIAL-QUALITY-STANDARD.md), [`SEO-EDITORIAL-AUTHORITY-STRATEGY.md`](SEO-EDITORIAL-AUTHORITY-STRATEGY.md).

---

## Most important rule

**Authority cannot be outsourced to generation systems.**

Published Intelligence must reflect **expert interpretation** and institutional judgment. AI may support process; it does not replace the author function for authority content.

---

## Allowed (assistive)

When used with human oversight and final accountability:

- **Research assistance** — locating sources, dates, public records pointers (verify everything).  
- **Structure exploration** — outlining options; human chooses and rewrites.  
- **Synthesis support** — clustering notes; human validates accuracy and nuance.  
- **Editing acceleration** — grammar, clarity, consistency (human signs off on substance).  
- **Operational drafting help** — first drafts **only** where a named expert rewrites for judgment, tone, and risk.

---

## Forbidden

- **AI-generated commodity articles** published as finished Intelligence.  
- **Scaled article generation** for cadence or keyword coverage.  
- **SEO article farming** — volume or variants driven primarily by search.  
- **Generic legal explainers** produced by model without expert rewrite.  
- **AI-written thought leadership** presented as firm judgment without meaningful human authorship.  
- **Publishing without expert interpretation** — no institutional stake in the take.

---

## Quality and risk checks

- Final copy must pass the **sophistication check** in the quality standard (non-obvious value for a serious reader).  
- Factual claims, quotes, and legal characterisations require **human verification** — models hallucinate.  
- Tone must not drift into **AI-generic** balance, filler, or faux neutrality on matters requiring position.  
- Any compliance, privilege, or jurisdictional sensitivity: **human-only** gate — not a model decision.

---

## Disclosure and process

- Internal workflows should record **human author accountability** for published pieces (existing bylines and editorial process prevail).  
- If firm policy later requires explicit reader-facing disclosure for certain assistive uses, align this document with partnership and compliance — do not pre-empt firm legal advice here.
