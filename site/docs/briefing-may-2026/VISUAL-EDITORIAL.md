# Visual Editorial

This document defines emotional and editorial visual behavior for the six-page sequence. It defines role and intent, not mechanics.

## 1. Why does digital representation matter commercially?

**Emotional tone:** Quiet institutional confidence.  
**Visual purpose:** Establish seriousness and strategic intent at first glance.  
**Image dominance:** Low to moderate, with typography carrying opening authority.  
**Compositional behavior:** Sparse and deliberate, with one clear visual anchor and substantial breathing room.  
**Asymmetry guidance:** Slight asymmetry to avoid presentation symmetry and preserve editorial character.  
**Crop philosophy:** Use imagery that suggests institutional context, not interface detail.  
**Whitespace behavior:** Generous whitespace to signal control and restraint.  
**What should visually disappear:** Presentation tropes, overt labels, and any sense of launch energy.  
**What the page should feel like:** A chairman's opening note, calm and externally credible.

## 2. How do practitioner profiles affect mandate confidence?

**Emotional tone:** Assured credibility with discretion.  
**Visual purpose:** Reinforce practitioner validation and professional standing.  
**Image dominance:** High, led by a single strong visual mass.  
**Compositional behavior:** One dominant anchor with a subordinate contextual counterpoint.  
**Asymmetry guidance:** Strong asymmetry to privilege credibility over comparison logic.  
**Crop philosophy:** Emphasize signals of authority and professional context; remove interface noise.  
**Whitespace behavior:** Controlled margins around anchors to preserve premium pacing.  
**What should visually disappear:** Browser-heavy framing and explanatory overlays.  
**What the page should feel like:** Credible, composed, and referral-safe.

## 3. How does expertise clarity improve enquiry quality?

**Emotional tone:** Structured clarity without visual urgency.  
**Visual purpose:** Communicate legibility of capability and ownership.  
**Image dominance:** High, with text acting as framing rather than interpretation.  
**Compositional behavior:** Hierarchy-first rhythm that implies order and scope.  
**Asymmetry guidance:** Balanced asymmetry that feels deliberate, not dramatic.  
**Crop philosophy:** Prioritize structural legibility and calm hierarchy over interface completeness.  
**Whitespace behavior:** Keep open zones that separate ideas and reduce cognitive load.  
**What should visually disappear:** Dense interface regions and any impression of feature demonstration.  
**What the page should feel like:** Clear, ordered, and commercially legible.

## 4. Why does institutional authority matter before contact?

**Emotional tone:** Composed authority and pre-contact trust.  
**Visual purpose:** Signal institutional consistency before interaction.  
**Image dominance:** Moderate to high, with one clear anchor supported by restrained text.  
**Compositional behavior:** Coherent and stable, conveying reliability rather than motion.  
**Asymmetry guidance:** Quiet asymmetry to maintain editorial sophistication.  
**Crop philosophy:** Select evidence that conveys confidence and discretion rather than interface breadth.  
**Whitespace behavior:** Preserve calm intervals that support perceived seriousness.  
**What should visually disappear:** Comparative theatrics and visual argumentation devices.  
**What the page should feel like:** Authoritative before contact, not assertive after explanation.

## 5. How does operational clarity support growth?

**Emotional tone:** Practical calm with commercial intent.  
**Visual purpose:** Show that pathways to engagement are clear and friction-aware.  
**Image dominance:** Moderate, enough to evidence clarity without becoming transactional.  
**Compositional behavior:** Stable and legible progression that implies guided movement.  
**Asymmetry guidance:** Mild asymmetry to avoid utility-sheet aesthetics.  
**Crop philosophy:** Focus on confidence in progression, not interface detail density.  
**Whitespace behavior:** Keep visible quiet space around the progression cue.  
**What should visually disappear:** Transactional interface feel and operational clutter.  
**What the page should feel like:** Calmly practical and commercially useful.

## 6. What does the platform now communicate about the firm?

**Emotional tone:** Final institutional composure.  
**Visual purpose:** Consolidate the cumulative belief in firm standard and mandate suitability.  
**Image dominance:** Low to moderate, with editorial tone carrying the close.  
**Compositional behavior:** Minimal and settled, with one final anchor of confidence.  
**Asymmetry guidance:** Subtle asymmetry only, preserving quiet closure.  
**Crop philosophy:** Use imagery only if it strengthens continuity of institutional signal.  
**Whitespace behavior:** Broad, calm whitespace to create a definitive end state.  
**What should visually disappear:** Closing-slide rhetoric, summary graphics, and persuasive flourishes.  
**What the page should feel like:** The inside back page of an institutional note, final and assured.
