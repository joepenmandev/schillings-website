# Primary thesis

Sophisticated clients and introducers evaluate firms digitally before first contact, and their confidence in authority, trust and discretion affects mandate quality; the platform structure is therefore positioned to represent the firm with greater institutional clarity at that decision point.

# Supporting commercial themes

## Practitioner credibility

Practitioner context is structured to make independent standing and professional positioning easier to validate.

## Expertise clarity

Expertise areas are represented with clearer ownership and scope, reducing ambiguity at first review.

## Authority reinforcement

The overall structure reinforces institutional signals through consistency of framing across key route families.

## Enquiry quality

Contact pathways are aligned to support more relevant, better-qualified enquiries.

## Referral confidence

Introducers can assess practitioner and capability context with less friction, supporting referral confidence.

## Institutional trust

Presentation choices are calibrated for discretion and consistency, reinforcing institutional trust.

## Operational clarity

Core pathways are structured to communicate next steps and practical contact routes more clearly.
