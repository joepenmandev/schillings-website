# Production Plan

This plan is operational. Layer 1 governs all decisions in Layer 2.

The compositor is not the primary thinking tool.

Composition may not reinterpret the thesis, rewrite strategic framing, invent new messaging, or introduce new structure.

## PHASE 1 — lock principles

**Objective:** Confirm the constitutional baseline for scope, tone, and non-goals.  
**Output:** Approved `PROJECT-CONSTRAINTS.md`, `THESIS.md`, and `BELIEF-PROGRESSION.md`.  
**Approval gate:** Written confirmation that Layer 1 is final.  
**Freeze point:** Layer 1 content is frozen and cannot be edited in downstream phases without explicit reset.  
**What NOT to do yet:** Do not draft spread copy or discuss composition behavior.

## PHASE 2 — approve narrative

**Objective:** Lock the six-page strategic narrative and belief sequence.  
**Output:** Approved `SPREAD-NARRATIVE.md`.  
**Approval gate:** Written confirmation that each page question and strategic paragraph are final.  
**Freeze point:** Page narrative is frozen before visual direction starts.  
**What NOT to do yet:** Do not choose imagery or begin compositor work.

## PHASE 3 — approve visual direction

**Objective:** Lock emotional and editorial behavior for each spread.  
**Output:** Approved `VISUAL-EDITORIAL.md`.  
**Approval gate:** Written confirmation that visual role aligns with narrative intent.  
**Freeze point:** Visual direction is frozen before image curation begins.  
**What NOT to do yet:** Do not define layout mechanics, coordinates, or implementation details.

## PHASE 4 — curate imagery

**Objective:** Select and approve supporting evidence plates under the locked narrative and visual direction.  
**Output:** Curated image set with approved crops and hierarchy intent.  
**Approval gate:** Written sign-off on each spread's primary and secondary evidence plate.  
**Freeze point:** Curated imagery is frozen before composition assembly.  
**What NOT to do yet:** Do not rewrite captions, strategic paragraphs, or page intent.

## PHASE 5 — compose spreads

**Objective:** Assemble the six spreads using frozen narrative, visual direction, and curated imagery.  
**Output:** First composed briefing draft for editorial review.  
**Approval gate:** Confirmation that composition reflects locked materials without reinterpretation.  
**Freeze point:** Composition structure is frozen pending print review comments only.  
**What NOT to do yet:** Do not introduce new strategic claims or structural variants.

## PHASE 6 — print review

**Objective:** Validate composure, legibility, and institutional tone in print conditions.  
**Output:** Print-reviewed draft with limited editorial adjustments.  
**Approval gate:** Confirmation that the document is externally forwardable in tone and quality.  
**Freeze point:** Typography, spacing rhythm, and visual density are frozen after print approval.  
**What NOT to do yet:** Do not reopen thesis, narrative sequence, or imagery selection.

## PHASE 7 — partner review/freeze

**Objective:** Complete partner-facing approval and finalize the briefing as a fixed institutional note.  
**Output:** Final approved briefing and archived process record.  
**Approval gate:** Partner approval to circulate.  
**Freeze point:** Final document and source set are frozen for controlled reuse only.  
**What NOT to do yet:** Do not start derivative versions or alternate narrative tracks.
