## Overall Assessment

The hidden benchmark successfully tests a safer, denser, more specific bio style within the shared Schillings profile system.  
It improves remit clarity and authority accumulation in copy, while preserving institutional consistency and restrained contact behavior.

Primary remaining gap: independent proof still appears below the biography because the benchmark uses the standard section order; first-screen trust is stronger than baseline prose quality but still not fully solved against the "early independent signal" principle.

---

## Evaluation Against Requested Questions

### 1) Does trust form earlier?

**Partial pass.**

- Trust forms earlier through a specific opening paragraph (role, stakeholder type, cross-jurisdiction pressure context).
- However, independent validation remains deferred to `PersonTrustSignals`, below biography and below fold on many mobile journeys.

Mapped references:

- Aligns with `SCHILLINGS-BIO-GAP-AUDIT.md` G1/G2 goals only partially.
- Still short of `BIO-IMPROVEMENT-PRINCIPLES.md` Principle 2 requirement for early independent signal.

### 2) Does authority accumulate?

**Pass.**

- Biography follows a clear arc:
  1) remit and evaluator relevance
  2) operating context and constraints
  3) representative matter texture
  4) external recognition / thought output context
- This reflects `PATTERN-LIBRARY.md` "Three-Stage Authority Arc" and "Paragraph Function Discipline."

### 3) Does it feel denser without clutter?

**Pass.**

- Copy is meaningfully denser than many current profiles and still readable.
- Information is segmented by paragraph function and avoids long testimonial dumps.
- Uses shared page structure, so density does not depend on decorative layout.

### 4) Does proof feel plain and credible?

**Mostly pass (with caveat).**

- Proof is plain/scannable via recognitions in `PersonTrustSignals`.
- Recognition mix is realistic for testing (directories + other).
- Caveat: proof is not adjacent to first major claims; this remains a sequencing gap.

### 5) Does portrait support rather than dominate?

**Pass.**

- Benchmark uses shared portrait treatment in `PersonProfileArticle`.
- Placeholder portrait supports identity confirmation; authority remains text/proof led.
- No bespoke visual art direction introduced.

### 6) Does biography feel inhabited and specific?

**Pass.**

- Includes concrete operating context (stakeholders, parallel decisions, cross-border conditions).
- Includes representative matter texture without confidential detail.
- Avoids generic prestige adjectives as primary authority mechanism.

### 7) Does contact feel restrained?

**Pass.**

- Contact and CTA remain shared, procedural, and separated from authority claims.
- Language remains calm and non-salesy, consistent with benchmark principles.

### 8) Does it still feel like Schillings?

**Pass.**

- Uses shared components, shared section order, shared typography/spacing behavior.
- Institutional voice stays aligned with existing profile system.
- No profile-specific layout branch or design-system divergence.
