# Change Examples

Purpose: canonical before/after examples for approved practitioner bio improvements.  
Use this as editor training material to reinforce institutional seriousness and anti-marketing discipline.

## 1) Weak vs Strong Bio Opening

### Weak
"A leading trusted adviser with exceptional expertise in complex matters."

### Strong
"Advises boards and founders on privacy and disputes where legal, communications, and intelligence decisions must align quickly across jurisdictions."

Why stronger:

- role and remit are specific
- operational context is explicit
- no unsupported prestige language

---

## 2) Promotional vs Operational Phrasing

### Promotional
"Delivers world-class outcomes for high-profile clients in high-stakes situations."

### Operational
"Leads multidisciplinary teams on cross-border reputation and dispute matters involving urgent publication risk, interim applications, and stakeholder coordination."

Why stronger:

- describes what is done, not just status
- adds matter texture without over-disclosure

---

## 3) Weak vs Improved Proof Labels

### Weak
"Awards"
"Recognition"
"More links"

### Improved
"Independent directories"
"Representative publications"
"Additional references"

Why stronger:

- clearer source intent
- easier evaluator scan
- less decorative framing

---

## 4) Weak vs Improved CTA Language

### Weak
"Get in touch now to work with our top experts."

### Improved
"To discuss whether Schillings can help, use our contact page. Nothing on this profile constitutes legal advice or a client relationship until terms are agreed."

Why stronger:

- procedural and restrained tone
- institutional seriousness over conversion pressure

---

## 5) Weak vs Improved Recognition Ordering

### Weak ordering
1. generic press mention
2. secondary list inclusion
3. major independent legal directory ranking

### Improved ordering
1. major independent legal directory ranking
2. secondary independent listing
3. context-relevant external contribution

Why stronger:

- prioritizes evaluator-relevant authority first
- reduces noise and improves scan confidence

---

## 6) Weak vs Improved Claim-Proof Pairing

### Weak
"Frequently handles cross-border matters."  
(proof appears much later in separate section)

### Improved
"Handles cross-border matters involving parallel publication risk and jurisdictional coordination; independent listings and representative matters are provided in the profile proof section."

Why stronger:

- connects claim to evidence path
- reduces trust gap between assertion and substantiation

---

## 7) Weak vs Improved Institutional Tone

### Weak
"A highly sought-after thought leader known for dynamic, game-changing strategy."

### Improved
"Provides strategic counsel on reputation risk and dispute exposure, with a focus on practical sequencing across legal, intelligence, and communications workstreams."

Why stronger:

- calm lexical temperature
- factual confidence
- institution-first voice

---

## Usage Rule

Examples in this file illustrate behavior, not copy to reuse verbatim.  
When updating live bios, preserve factual accuracy, institutional realism, and shared-system consistency.
