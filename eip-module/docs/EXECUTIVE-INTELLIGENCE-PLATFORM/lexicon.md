# Lexicon — Schillings Intelligence

*Working draft. v0.1.*

The platform uses a small, deliberately controlled vocabulary. Each term below has a single definition. Authors and analysts must use the terms as defined — alternative phrasings are tracked in the Voice and Tone Bible.

---

## Platform terms

**Schillings Intelligence**
The platform's product name. A premium sub-brand of Schillings.

**Digital Executive Infrastructure**
The category Schillings Intelligence is positioned within: software that powerful people, and the institutions around them, use as part of how they operate.

**Intelligence Report**
The unified output format produced by every assessment in the platform. Always rendered through the same renderer for cross-tool consistency.

**Executive Briefing PDF**
A board-grade, branded artifact exported from an Intelligence Report. Signed, timestamped, optionally partner-bylined. The most-shared object in the platform.

---

## Pillars

**Exposure Intelligence**
Privacy + AI + executive risk. Concerns the surface area of the principal across public data, AI systems, and adjacent vectors.

**Narrative Intelligence**
Media + reputation + communications. Concerns how the principal is interpreted, framed, and amplified.

**Crisis Intelligence**
Corporate + legal + operational. Concerns the trajectory and intervention points of incidents.

---

## Indexes (named methodologies)

**Executive Exposure Index**
The composite Index produced by the Executive Exposure Audit. Eight sub-indices.

**Public Interpretation Analysis**
The output of the Headline Risk Assessment. Plural by design — interpretation has many lenses.

**Narrative Escalation Map**
The output of the Narrative Warfare Analyzer. Predictive intelligence assessment of how narratives evolve.

**Crisis Escalation Forecast**
The output of the Reputation Crisis Simulator. Boardroom-grade simulation of 72-hour crisis dynamics.

**Executive AI Misuse Risk**
The composite Index produced by the AI Impersonation Exposure Audit.

---

## Trust and provenance

**Confidence band**
The level of certainty held in an Index, sub-index, dimension, or paragraph. Always shown alongside a score or assessment.

**Confidence panel**
A required element of every Intelligence Report. Discloses the confidence band, named assumptions, and the largest unknown.

**AI provenance disclosure**
A required panel showing which signals in a report are deterministic, model-inferred, or analyst-reviewed.

**Provenance trail**
The signed lineage attached to every exported report: inputs hash, model version, methodology version, analyst signature (where applicable), timestamp, expiry.

**Methodology version**
Each Index has a versioned methodology specification (e.g., v1.0). Public methodology pages display the version, peer reviewers, and change log.

**Peer review**
External, named domain authority who reviews and endorses a methodology before it carries the version label without the `-draft` suffix.

---

## People and tiers

**Principal**
The person whose risk is being assessed. The platform produces personalised assessments only for the requesting principal, or for a third party whose authorised representative has attested to consent.

**Verified Principal**
A principal who has completed a one-time identity verification and unlocks the deeper personalisation tier.

**Authorised representative**
A staff member, family-office officer, or named advisor who attests to consent on a principal's behalf for assessments concerning that principal.

**Founding client**
A member of the invitation-only cohort active before public launch. Provides calibration and testimonial value.

**Analyst review**
A human review of an Intelligence Report by a named Schillings analyst, returned within the published SLA. Optionally partner-bylined.

**Partner-bylined**
An Intelligence Report or Executive Briefing PDF co-signed by a named Schillings partner who has personally reviewed the assessment.

---

## Content and assessment terms

**Signal**
A discrete observation or measurement that contributes to a sub-index. Always tied to a source, a method, and a confidence value.

**Sub-index**
A named scoring component of a composite Index (e.g., AI Visibility within the Executive Exposure Index).

**Adversary archetype**
A named archetype from the Threat Actor Library. Always an archetype — never an identification of a named individual or organisation.

**Adversary lens**
A view of an Intelligence Report rendered through one or more adversary archetypes.

**Recommended priorities**
Operational actions framed in imperative voice. Never legal advice. Never advice on regulatory enforcement.

**Linked insight**
A cross-tool recommendation of a next assessment, with the explicit reason rendered in-UI.

---

## Operational terms

**Crisis War-Room Mode**
A live-incident variant of the Crisis Escalation Forecast. Gated behind contract and SLA. Not self-serve.

**Continuous Monitoring**
A subscription tier that converts one-time scans into a monitored profile with alerts when new exposure signals appear.

**Refusal pattern**
A documented way the platform declines a request that falls outside its principled boundaries. Always explains the principle and offers an alternative path.

**AI red-line agent**
A dedicated last-mile compliance gate. Reviews every model-generated output against the Output Safety Taxonomy and the Voice and Tone Bible before display.

**Schillings Intelligence Corpus**
The proprietary mix of methodology, vetted public-records pipelines, partner-licensed signals, and (anonymised, opt-in) network signals that gives the platform its data moat.

**State of Executive Risk report**
The annual Schillings publication anchored in (anonymised) platform signals. Defines the category once a year.

**Quarterly intelligence brief**
A short, high-craft Schillings-authored digest published quarterly to clients and trusted press contacts.
