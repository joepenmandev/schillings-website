# Voice and Tone Bible

*Working draft. v0.1.*

The platform's most fragile asset is its voice. Without a codified standard, AI-generated outputs drift toward generic SaaS tone, undoing the premium positioning. This document is the source of truth for everyone — writers, analysts, engineers building prompts, designers reviewing copy.

---

## Voice principles (commandments)

1. **Restrained, never sensational.** We describe risk; we do not catastrophise it.
2. **Precise, never vague.** Every claim is grounded in a signal, an assumption, or a clearly labelled inference.
3. **Direct, never ornate.** No throat-clearing. No corporate hedging. No empty introductions.
4. **Authoritative, never authoritarian.** We share judgement; we do not impose it.
5. **Calm, never alarmist.** The tone of a senior advisor at midnight, not a press release at noon.
6. **Specific, never generic.** Concrete signals, named adversary archetypes, real timelines — not abstractions.
7. **Humble about uncertainty.** Confidence is a feature, not a vulnerability. We name what we don't know.
8. **Aware of consequence.** Every word will be read by someone whose reputation, family, or company is at stake.

## Tone modulation by context

| Surface | Tone register |
|---|---|
| Manifesto | Considered, purposeful, slightly literary. The voice of a thinking firm. |
| Methodology pages | Technical, transparent, peer-reviewed-paper energy. |
| Tool result summary | Briefing-grade. Concise. The first sentence delivers the verdict. |
| Confidence and assumptions panel | Plain, factual, almost mechanical. |
| Recommended priorities | Operational. Imperative voice ("Tighten…", "Confirm…"). Never advisory in the legal sense. |
| Analyst review intake | Professional, discreet, low-friction. The voice of a private banker. |
| Scenario library | Cinematic, controlled, taut. Not theatrical. |
| Footers and disclaimers | Honest, plain. No legalistic obscurity. |

## Voice DOs and DON'Ts

### DO

- Lead with the verdict.
- Use specific timeframes ("within 24 hours") not vague ones ("soon").
- Use named adversary archetypes from the Threat Actor Library.
- Show the underlying signal whenever a score is presented.
- Acknowledge uncertainty explicitly.
- Use plain English. Reading age 14, not 9.
- Use sentence case for headings.
- Default to active voice.

### DON'T

- Use "AI-powered", "next-generation", "cutting-edge", "revolutionary", "game-changing", or any synonym thereof.
- Use "leverage", "synergy", "ideation", "actionable insights", "deep dive", "world-class".
- Use exclamation marks. Anywhere.
- Use emojis. Anywhere.
- Promise outcomes you cannot guarantee.
- Use legal terminology in a way that implies advice.
- Compare clients to celebrities.
- Refer to the platform as "tool" — it is a *platform*, *assessment*, or *report*.

## Output structure templates

### Intelligence Report — opening line

> "[Index name] places [subject] in the [band] band, driven primarily by [top signal] and [second signal]."

### Intelligence Report — confidence statement

> "This assessment is held at [low / moderate / high] confidence. The largest unknown is [assumption]."

### Recommended priorities — opening line

> "Three actions, in order of urgency."

### Analyst review CTA — invitation

> "If this assessment intersects an active matter, request analyst review. A named Schillings partner will return signed observations within [SLA]."

## Examples — before and after

### Top-line summary

**Bad:**
> "Our AI-powered analysis has detected concerning levels of risk that may impact your reputation in unforeseen ways."

**Good:**
> "The Executive Exposure Index places this profile in the elevated band, driven by deep public-data exposure and an unusually high AI Visibility score."

### Confidence statement

**Bad:**
> "Our analysis is highly accurate."

**Good:**
> "Held at moderate confidence. Largest unknown: whether the historical interview content has been ingested into the major foundation models."

### Recommended priority

**Bad:**
> "We recommend you take immediate action to leverage best-in-class privacy protocols."

**Good:**
> "Within 48 hours: tighten the personal-data trail surfaced in signals 3 and 7. Engage the Privacy practice if a regulator is plausibly involved."

## Operationalisation

- All AI prompts must include the full Voice Principles section as a system message.
- All AI outputs are validated by the **AI red-line agent** before display, including a tone check.
- Editorial owns the Voice Bible. Updates are versioned and announced internally.
- Methodology authors must demonstrate voice fluency before authoring an Index page.
- Analyst-reviewed addenda must conform to this bible verbatim.
