## What it answers

- How available is my public media footprint for voice and image replication?
- Which misuse scenarios are most plausible for my profile?
- Which controls should be prioritised first?
- What is the largest uncertainty in this assessment?

---

## What it produces

An **Executive AI Misuse Risk** Index containing six sub-indices:

- Voice cloning risk.
- Facial replication exposure.
- Impersonation attractiveness.
- Fraud susceptibility.
- Public media availability.
- AI attack surface.

Plus three to five named attack plausibility scenarios drawn from archetype-based scenario patterns.

---

## Inputs

- Name, role, and organisation profile.
- Optional: interview and media URLs.
- Optional: known media archive references.
- Optional: staff or family-office consent flags.

The Assessment analyses availability characteristics and does not generate or store synthetic media.

---

## What you receive

A unified Schillings Intelligence Report containing:

- Top-line Index band and highest-contributing sub-indices.
- Sub-index breakdown.
- Attack plausibility scenarios.
- Confidence panel.
- AI provenance disclosure.
- Recommended priorities focused on verification and instruction controls.
- Linked insights to Exposure and Crisis assessments.
- Analyst review CTA.

## How it connects

- → Run the **Executive Exposure Audit** for full principal exposure posture.

---

## Privacy and refusal

- Inputs are not retained beyond the session by default.
- The Assessment refuses requests to create synthetic media.
- The Assessment refuses third-party assessments without authorised-representative consent.
- The Assessment refuses requests involving minors.
