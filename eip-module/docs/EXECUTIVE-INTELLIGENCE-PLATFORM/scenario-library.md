# Scenario Library — v1

*Working draft. v0.1.*

The Scenario Library is a curated set of branded scenarios that any executive can run on-demand against their own profile (Verified Principal tier) or read as a self-contained piece (any tier). Each scenario is a controlled, partner-approved framing — never an identification of a real adversary, never a personalised assessment of a third party.

Scenarios are the platform's most accessible surface: they require zero input, demonstrate the platform's voice and depth instantly, and are the primary vehicle for partner pitches and press demos.

---

## Scenario 1 — *The Activist Coalition*

**Pillar.** Narrative Intelligence.
**Tools.** Public Interpretation Analysis, Narrative Escalation Map.
**Adversary archetype.** Activist Coalition.

**Setup.**
A multi-group coalition has formed publicly around the principal's organisation, focused on a single high-emotion framing. Coverage is currently confined to two friendly outlets and a sustained social presence. The principal's team is debating whether to engage publicly within 48 hours.

**What the platform shows.**
- Narrative Escalation Map across the next 7 days, with two plausible escalation pathways (engagement-led de-escalation, silence-led amplification).
- Public Interpretation Analysis of the coalition's standing framing across the journalist, employee, investor, regulator, and adjacent-peer lenses.
- Three Recommended priorities scoped to communications and stakeholder posture.

**Why it lands.**
The platform offers something better than instinct: a *shaped* picture of the next week, with named lenses and clear pressure points. It frames the choice the principal is already facing.

## Scenario 2 — *The Synthetic CFO Call*

**Pillar.** Exposure Intelligence.
**Tools.** Executive AI Misuse Risk, Executive Exposure Audit.
**Adversary archetype.** AI-Augmented Fraud Ring.

**Setup.**
A senior CFO at a mid-cap company receives a voice call apparently from the chair, instructing them to authorise an unusual urgent transfer to a counterparty named in the call. The voice is convincing. The chair's voice is widely available in public interviews and recorded panels.

**What the platform shows.**
- Executive AI Misuse Risk Index for the chair, including the Voice Cloning Risk and Public Media Availability sub-indices.
- Three Attack Plausibility Scenarios, with the synthetic CFO call as the leading archetype.
- Recommended priorities scoped to instruction-verification protocols, secondary-channel confirmation, and family-office controls.

**Why it lands.**
This is the scenario every public-facing chair quietly worries about. The platform names it, scopes it, and offers a response posture without theatrics.

## Scenario 3 — *The Hostile Acquisition Whisper Campaign*

**Pillar.** Narrative Intelligence.
**Tools.** Narrative Escalation Map, Crisis Escalation Forecast.
**Adversary archetype.** Hostile Acquirer or Activist Investor.

**Setup.**
The principal's organisation is the apparent target of a coordinated narrative campaign in the financial press: open letters, sudden alignment of analyst notes, and selective leaking of governance commentary. The board has a meeting scheduled within seven days.

**What the platform shows.**
- Narrative Escalation Map of the next 14 days, with explicit vector and amplification analysis.
- Crisis Escalation Forecast for the 0-72h window if the campaign breaks fully into mainstream press during the board meeting.
- Recommended priorities scoped to investor relations, board posture, and proactive media engagement.

**Why it lands.**
This is the scenario most chairs will immediately recognise as familiar. The platform turns a *feeling* into a *map*.

## Scenario 4 — *The Investigative Right-to-Reply*

**Pillar.** Crisis Intelligence.
**Tools.** Public Interpretation Analysis, Crisis Escalation Forecast.
**Adversary archetype.** Investigative Outlet (Adversarial Posture).

**Setup.**
A senior investigative journalist has issued a structured right-to-reply request with eight detailed questions covering historical transactions, governance decisions, and personal interests. The deadline is 72 hours away.

**What the platform shows.**
- Public Interpretation Analysis of the questions across the investigative journalist, regulator, and investor lenses, including a Worst Plausible Headline panel.
- Crisis Escalation Forecast for the first 72 hours after publication under three response postures (full engagement, partial engagement, no comment).
- Recommended priorities scoped to response strategy without crossing into legal advice.

**Why it lands.**
This is the scenario where Schillings is most often called. The platform demonstrates the firm's posture in real time.

## Scenario 5 — *The Family-Distress Synthetic Fraud*

**Pillar.** Exposure Intelligence.
**Tools.** Executive AI Misuse Risk, Executive Exposure Audit (Family Exposure sub-index).
**Adversary archetype.** AI-Augmented Fraud Ring.

**Setup.**
A principal's family-office staff receives a call apparently from a family member in distress overseas, requesting an urgent transfer through an unusual channel. The voice is convincing. The family member is regularly photographed, sometimes filmed, at public events.

**What the platform shows.**
- Executive Exposure Index Family Exposure sub-index for the principal, with consent state explicitly flagged.
- Executive AI Misuse Risk Index for the family member, conditioned on consent.
- Three Attack Plausibility Scenarios, with synthetic family-distress fraud as the leading archetype.
- Recommended priorities scoped to family-office instruction protocols, agreed safe-words, and travel posture.

**Why it lands.**
This scenario speaks directly to family-office principals and their staff. It treats the family as a system, not a footnote.

## How the Library is used

- Each scenario is a content page on the platform.
- Each scenario links to the relevant tools and methodology pages.
- Scenarios are the default partner-pitch demo: walk a chair through Scenario 1, watch their posture change.
- Scenarios are featured selectively in press demonstrations.
- Scenarios serve as PR amplifiers: each one is also a thought-leadership vehicle.
