# The Schillings Intelligence Manifesto

*Working draft. v0.1.*

---

## A new operating environment for powerful people

The world's most exposed individuals — chairs, founders, principals, families, and the institutions that surround them — operate inside a risk environment that did not exist five years ago.

Reputation is no longer narrated by the press. It is *constructed in real time* by an unbounded set of actors: anonymous accounts, activist coalitions, opportunistic fraudsters, AI-generated content, hostile state-aligned operators, and a fragmenting media estate that rewards speed over verification.

Privacy has stopped being a policy question. It is a *systems* question — about which databases hold which data, which models have been trained on which signals, which platforms render which content, and how all of this can be aggregated into an accurate picture of someone's life by a stranger with sufficient motivation.

Crisis has stopped being an event. It is a *posture*. The first 72 hours of an incident now compress into the first 72 *minutes*, and the response infrastructure inside most organisations was built for an older clock.

This is the environment Schillings has worked in, on behalf of clients, for forty years.

This is the environment for which we are now building a new kind of infrastructure.

---

## What we are building

We are building **Digital Executive Infrastructure** — software that powerful people, and the teams around them, use as part of how they operate.

Not a chatbot. Not a calculator. Not an analyser. Not a marketing tool.

A *platform*. Three pillars: Exposure, Narrative, Crisis. Five named methodologies, each authored, peer-reviewed, and versioned. One unified Intelligence Report. One coherent point of view.

The platform answers the questions that, until now, only Schillings could answer:

- *How exposed am I, really?*
- *What would a journalist, a regulator, an activist, an investor — read into this?*
- *Which narratives are forming around me, and where are they likely to escalate?*
- *If something happens tonight, what does the next 72 hours look like?*
- *What does the AI ecosystem already think it knows about me — and what could it be made to fabricate?*

The answers are not opinions. They are *intelligence assessments*. Signal-based. Confidence-banded. Provenance-trailed. Reviewed, where it matters, by named Schillings partners.

---

## What we are not building

We are not building hype.

We will not name third parties as adversaries.
We will not provide legal advice through an interface.
We will not generate content that pretends to be more certain than it is.
We will not collect more data than is strictly required, or hold it longer than is strictly necessary.
We will not let the technology run ahead of the people responsible for what it produces.

We are not building speed at the cost of judgement.

The platform's most premium feature is a human being.

---

## Our point of view

There is a category of risk that does not show up on a balance sheet, but that determines whether a chair survives the next acquisition, whether a founder retains the trust of their board, whether a principal can travel without being followed, whether a family can attend a public event without consequence.

This category — *modern executive risk* — is what we now understand better than anyone else.

The platform makes that understanding accessible without making it generic. It scales without becoming shallow. It uses AI without abandoning judgement. It produces beautiful artifacts without becoming theatrical.

It treats the people it serves with the seriousness they deserve.

## Who this is for

- Chairs and CEOs of listed companies whose personal exposure shapes corporate exposure.
- Founders whose personal life and professional reputation are inseparable.
- Family principals and family offices managing risk across multiple generations and jurisdictions.
- General counsels and chief communications officers whose role is to anticipate, not just react.
- Advisors — wealth, comms, security — who need infrastructure that matches their clients' expectations.

It is not for everyone. It is not designed to be.

## What good looks like

- A Schillings Intelligence Report should feel inevitable — like the answer was already known and the platform simply rendered it.
- A Briefing PDF should be the artifact a chair quietly forwards to their CFO at midnight.
- An analyst review should be the moment a client decides Schillings is part of how they operate, not a phone call they make in extremis.
- A scenario should feel rehearsed, not invented.
- A confidence score should be trusted, because every assumption underneath it is visible.

## A note on the future

The risk environment will get faster, stranger, and more synthetic. AI will accelerate impersonation. State-aligned actors will continue to weaponise narrative. Regulation will lag, then over-correct. The boundary between personal and corporate exposure will continue to dissolve.

Schillings has spent forty years preparing for this moment. The platform is how we make that preparation available to the people who need it now.

The tools are not the product.
The feeling is the product.
The feeling is *understood*.
